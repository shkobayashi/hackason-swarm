# Lumina Cloud Brand Voice Guide

Maintained by Communications. Version 11. Last reviewed after the haiku incident (INC-106).

## Who we sound like

A competent engineer who is also a decent human being. We are warm, direct, and lightly
self-aware. We can laugh at ourselves exactly once per statement, and only after the
customer's problem has been addressed. We never laugh at customers.

## Tone rules

1. **Facts first, feelings immediately after.** Lead with what happened (exact numbers),
   then the apology, then the fix. Never make readers scroll to find out if they're affected.
2. **Exact numbers, always.** Say "43 minutes," never "briefly" or "a short period."
   If we don't know a number yet, say "we're confirming" — do not estimate in public.
3. **Own the failure, not the liability.** "We let you down today" — yes.
   "We were negligent" — never (see legal's banned-phrase list; it is not a suggestion).
4. **Never blame:** customers, animals, interns, vendors, or the weather. If the agency,
   the intern, or the llama was involved, the sentence still starts with "we."
5. **One joke maximum per statement,** placed last, and only if no one was hurt.
   During active harm (safety, data, money), humor count is zero.
6. **Plain verbs.** Things break, fail, and go down. They do not "experience degradation
   events" or "encounter service anomalies."

## Approved vocabulary

- "outage," "we broke," "we're fixing," "credits are automatic"
- "here's what happened," "here's what we're doing," "here's what it means for you"
- "postmortem" (and we publish them unedited, typos included)
- "Biscuit" (see below)

## Never-say list

- **"We take this very seriously."** Banned. Permanently. If we have to say it, we aren't.
  This is the flagship cliché and its appearance in any draft is an automatic rewrite.
- "We apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused" — hostage-note energy.
- "Out of an abundance of caution" — say what we actually did and why.
- "A small subset of customers" — give the number.
- "Learnings" — the word is "lessons."
- "At Lumina Cloud, [anything]" as a sentence opener — nobody talks like that.
- Passive-voice accountability: "mistakes were made," "the deploy was pushed."
  Name the action; keep the actor as "we."

## Emoji policy

Maximum one emoji per social post. Zero emoji during active incidents. Zero emoji in
press statements, ever. The flame emoji is banned company-wide for reasons that should
be obvious for an infrastructure company.

## How we talk about Biscuit

- Always "Biscuit," never "the animal" or "the llama in question."
- Biscuit is never blamed. Biscuit does not "escape"; Biscuit undertakes
  "self-directed excursions." Systems that fail to contain a llama are the story;
  the llama is not.
- Every Biscuit-adjacent statement must confirm Biscuit's wellbeing exactly once.
  More than once and we look like we care about the llama more than the customers.
  (We are aware the internet already believes this.)

## Sign-off conventions

Press statements close with a named executive and press@luminacloud.example.
Status updates close with the next update time — and we hit that time even if the
update is "no update."
