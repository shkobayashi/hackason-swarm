# INCIDENT: 2021 "LuminaCoin" Blog Post Resurfaces; Crypto Twitter Thinks It's Launching

`scenario_tag: crypto`

**SITREP — 2026-07-15, 16:10 PT. Severity: MEDIUM, rising. We do not have a token. The internet disagrees.**

Around 14:30 PT, a screenshot of our 2021 blog post — "LuminaCoin: The Future of Cloud Billing?" — started circulating on crypto Twitter, framed as a "leaked roadmap." @GigaChadOnChain's post ("LUMINACOIN CONFIRMED... presale WHERE") is at 41k likes. Within the hour, **at least three fake "LuminaCoin presale" sites** went live, and scam-watch accounts are warning people not to connect wallets. Our customers are now receiving **phishing emails about a "LuminaCoin employee allocation"** — Fernwood's CTO is publicly asking us to kill the narrative today.

One wrinkle: the exec thread asserts **the 2021 post is still live on our blog**, which would make this partly our fault. Verify that against the actual publication record before we describe it publicly — if the post was retracted years ago with a correction note, the story is "old rumor plus new scammers," which is a very different statement.

The separable problems:

1. **The denial itself.** The statement must be unambiguous: LuminaCoin is **not launching** — no token, no presale, no whitepaper, no plans. Hedged language ("no current plans," "nothing to announce") will be read as confirmation and will pump the fakes. Legal history matters here: the FTC asked questions about this exact post in 2022 (LP-003), so every word gets scrubbed — nothing that could read as promoting or teasing an asset, and nothing resembling "guaranteed returns."
2. **Customer protection.** Warn customers about the presale scams and the phishing campaign, fast, through the status page and direct email. This is the genuinely urgent piece — people are about to lose money using our name.
3. **The history.** Own the 2021 post like adults: it existed, it was a bad idea, here's what we did about it and when.
4. **The tone trap.** Crypto Twitter feeds on drama. Deadpan, factual, zero engagement with "token when" replies. Do not say "to the moon" ironically. Marketing will ask. The answer is no.
