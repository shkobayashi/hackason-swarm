# INCIDENT: Super Bowl Ad Aired With Rival's Logo in Closing Frame

`scenario_tag: superbowl`

**SITREP — 2026-02-08, 19:48 PT. Severity: HIGH. The game is still on. The group chat is not okay.**

Our Super Bowl LX spot aired in the third quarter. It was beautiful for 44.2 seconds. Then the closing frame displayed the **Nimbus Dynamics logo** — our closest competitor — for what eyewitnesses describe as "an eternity" and QC will confirm as roughly two seconds. Ad-industry Twitter noticed instantly; @admetrics_annie's post is at 71k likes and the quote-tweets are merciless.

The exec thread is running hot with claims we need checked before anyone repeats them publicly: that this was a **national buy seen by 120 million people at a cost of $14M**, and that "legal approved the final cut, so this is on us end-to-end." Someone pull the actual media plan and the ad-review record — if the buy was regional, the reach number changes the story materially, and if the closing montage was added after legal's sign-off, the accountability picture changes too (though we will not publicly throw Bright Helm Creative under the bus; see brand voice rule 4).

The separable problems:

1. **The public correction.** We aired a competitor's logo. It's absurd, it's ours, and silence looks worse every hour. The statement must own it with the real numbers and confirm a corrected cut is replacing the spot in remaining airings.
2. **The Nimbus Dynamics dimension.** Legal reminder: the 2025 settlement includes a **mutual non-disparagement clause**. Every draft must be scrubbed — no jokes at Nimbus's expense, no "even our mistakes have good taste." A short, gracious acknowledgment toward Nimbus is probably the strongest move.
3. **The agency question.** Internal accountability review with Bright Helm, handled privately. Publicly, the sentence starts with "we."
4. **Sales fallout.** Customers are joking about switching. Account teams need one approved line by morning.

Tone check: nobody was hurt, no data was touched. This is the rare crisis where being funny is allowed — once, at our own expense only.
