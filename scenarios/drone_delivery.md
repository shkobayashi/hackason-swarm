# INCIDENT: Marketing Drone Delivers 400 T-Shirts to Courthouse Lawn Mid-Trial

`scenario_tag: drone`

**SITREP — 2026-07-15, 13:27 PT. Severity: HIGH. A judge is involved. The judge is not amused.**

This morning, a **Lumina AirDrop** marketing drone was supposed to deliver Summit swag — 400 "Shine On" t-shirts — to the HQ lawn for attendees. Instead, a mis-set waypoint sent it to the **Multnomah County Courthouse lawn, 2.1 miles away, during an active trial**. Deliveries continued for hours before anyone stopped it. The trial went into recess; local reporter Kim's post quoting the judge "considering contempt options for the sky" is at 88k likes, and two news helicopters filmed the lawn, which now looks like a very cheerful crime scene.

A reporter is running with a claim that **the drone was unregistered and the pilot unlicensed**. If our program records say otherwise, we need those facts in hand within the hour — that claim is the difference between "embarrassing mishap" and "rogue illegal drone operation" in tomorrow's coverage. Also unclear: how one drone delivered 400 shirts. Physics has questions; pull the flight logs.

The separable problems:

1. **The court.** A direct, formal apology to the court and coordination with county officials on cleanup — today, before any cute tweet. Disrupting a trial is not content.
2. **The regulator.** We received an FAA warning letter after the fountain incident in January (INC-108, LP-007), and a second deviation inside 24 months carries real consequences. Legal says: self-report promptly, and announce that the AirDrop program is **paused** pending our own review and full cooperation with the FAA. Do not speculate about fault, do not call it an "act of god," and do not let marketing call it "an unplanned brand activation."
3. **The process failure.** How did the waypoint clear the two-person review we added after January? Someone check the review log — quietly.
4. **The memes.** The internet is having a wonderful day. Our statement should be contrite toward the court, factual about the FAA, and save any warmth for the cleanup crew.
